Frames

What are frames?

Frames are absolutley esstential when it comes to fighting games, Pros use this data to learn how to counter their opponents every move, and optimize their play to the fullest

Types of Frames:

  • Startup: the number of frames it takes for the move to hit an opponent. Startup Frames are the type of frames that begin a move animation

    • Example: if you and your opponent press these buttons on the exact same frame, you would hit your opponent rather than the opposite due to the fact that your 9 frame move started up faster than their 11 frame start up frame move

  • Active: the number of frames the move can hit an oppponent. The frames that occur when startup frames finish

    • Example: Kano's F4(Wheel Kick) has 4 active frames, once the move starts up in 25 frames, the move will be able to hit the opponent for 4 frames,depending on this move hits an opponent, is blocked, or whiffs will determine what kind of frames will begin once the active frames are done.

  • Recovery: the number of frames the move takes to recover

  • Hit Advantage: the number of frames that the player can input a button press before an opponent

  • Block Advantage: the number of frames that the player can input a button before or after an opponent depending on if the move is plus or minus frames

  • Flawless Block Advantage: the number of frames that the player can input a button before or after a FLAWLESS BLOCK

Why Button Mashing doesn't work

    MK11 Lingo:

  • 50/50: A situation where an opponent is forced to guess between two mixups.

  • Anti-Air: a move that counters a jump/jump in

  • Chip Damage: damage a player recieves from an opponent while blocking

  • Corner: the two spots at the end of each side of the stage

  • DOT Damage: Damage Over Time, damage that drains health over time.

  • Fatal Blow: a powerful move that is avaliable once a player's lifebar falls below 30% that is unbreakable and causes major damage , can only be used once per match on hit

  • Krushing Blow(KB): a move that triggers if certain requirements are met, can lead to launchers, DOT damage, or extra damage.

  • Low Attack: an attack that must be blocked low to be suucessfully blocked

  • Neutral Game: where neither player has advanatge over another. Both trying to find an opening

  • Overhead Attack: an attack that must be blocked high to be successfully blocked

  • Parry: a specific move that counters an opponents attack

  • Punish: attacking an opponent who is currently in minus frames(unable to input a button)

  • Roll:used to roll forward or backward to get better positioning on an opponent a roll that is invincibleto all moves except for throws.

  • Throw:an attack that grabs the opponent and is unblockable, can be ducked or jumped out of for a punish

  • Wakeup/Getup:a move made when a knocked down player gets out of minus frames

  • Whiff: when an attack misses an opponent

  • Whiff Punish: when a player punishes an opponent when the opponent whiffs an attack
